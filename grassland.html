The Grassland Ecosystem

  • Grassland ecosystems are areas dominated by grasses and other plants that interact with their environment, including the soil, climate, and other living organisms.
  • There are many types of grasslands around the world, including prairies (found in North America), savannas (found in Africa and South America), and steppes (found in Asia and Europe).
  • Grasslands are home to a variety of animals, including bison and pronghorns in North American. Zebras and giraffes in African savannas, and wild horses and wolves in European steppes.
  • These ecosystems are able to help regulate the Earth's climate by storing carbon in the soil.
