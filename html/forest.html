The Forest Ecosystem

  • Forest ecosystems are areas dominated by trees and other woody plants that interact with their environment, including the soil, climate, and other living organisms.
  • There are many types of forests around the world, including tropical rainforests (found in South America, Africa, and Southeast Asia), boreal forests (also known as taiga, found in Canada, Russia, and Scandinavia), and temperate forests (found in North America, Europe, and Asia).
  • Forests are home to a diverse range of animals, including chimpanzees and gorillas in tropical rainforests, moose and wolves in boreal forests, and black bears and deer in temperate forests.
  • Forests are essential to the Earth's health. They provide oxygen, absorb carbon dioxide, and play a crucial role in regulating the Earth's climate.
